My Screenprinting Project!

In the screen printing project, we came up with a design to screen print onto fabric. Depending on your prefered design, it varied in how many seperate screens there were. Firstly, we were required to come up with a deisgn and assess its dimensions and color. After this, we needed to cut wood depending on our dimensions. In my case, i had to cut 2 14 inch peices and 2 11 inch peices to mkae 1 out of the 2 screens. Next, we had to attach then using staple guns and also attaching the first layer of fabric. Then, we actually printed the deisgn on sticker and centered the deisgn to our best ability. My 2 screens were centered well. Moreover, after the entire scren was finished, it was time to print onto fabric and boxes. I had to line up my 2 deisgns on top of each other to make it like my oriignal deisgn. This also depended on how many screens you had. All in all, this project taught me the usage of materials like hammers, staple guns, and elemnts like adobe.

The steps to completion!

  1. Pick design
  2. Assess number of screens needed
    3. Measure the needed wood size and create a digital overview on adobe illustrator
  3. Cut wood to these lengths and attach them using stapler gun to create a frame(2 if needed)
  4. Attach layer of fabric on the wood tightly
    5. Print designs on sticker, peel and attach to center of fabric on wood.
  5. Screenprint using paint color of choice on paper, fabric and boxes
  6. Finally, clean the frame using water to avoid staining.
