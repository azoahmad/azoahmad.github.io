Mystery Black Box Investigation

What is in the mystery black box? This was the goal of our project, to replicate the black box to find similar results to the real thing. The black box does not just contain tubes and containers but something more complex. We can make this assumption because of the compared results. Our class data shows that a different amount of water came out each time. Sometimes it came out more, sometimes less, and sometimes it was similar. For example, in one trial we imported 1000 mL and 1710 mL of water came out. Another trial showed that we imported 1000 mL and 370 mL came out. And finally we imported 1000 mL and 980 mL of water came out. This data was the original black box data. Our replica showed only similar results, these compared results led us to assume that the original and our replica do not have similar materials but something more complex. Our model was useful to us because it helped us visualize and construct our own model, helping us imagine and test for real. Our model consisted of tubes, cardboard, funnel, and containers. My claim at the start is supported by the evidence. This is because the evidence, which are the results, show that the original and model cannot have the same interior. The original had varying results and the model had results that stayed the same, or around the same. We did not succeed in replicating both box results. Some may say that both boxes are similar, to prove this, the evidence must support it. In our case it did not support this counter claim. In conclusion, we can infer that the black box differs from the model we constructed. This is because of the difference in results, proving my claim to be supported. It also proves that the boxes differ in their interiors.

Photography

As we photographed, we used the rule of thirds. The rule of thirds is a method that divides the photo into 2 horizontal and 2 vertical lines. This technique makes the image more balanced and appealing. It also can highlight certain things in the picture or enhance the mood/setting. During our construction process, we photographed our stages. Some taken from up top, some from the side, and even from a bottom point of view. In the assignment, I attached 3 photos displaying our use of the rule of thirds and our process.

Brainstorming

My initial brainstorming was that it consisted of tubes and such but I was stuck on how the results varied. My photoshop work showed a vertical placed box with interconnecting tubes, and containers to drain the water. My animation showed the water passing through it and exiting through the final tube. To come to this, I utilized many aspects of Adobe Photoshop. Some aspects are , the drawing tool, animations, coloring, and a trick to create a crumpled paper background to make the animation more appealing.

3d Maya

In Maya, I modeled all parts of the black box, such as the box, the cup, tubes, containers, personal hand, box door, funnels, and other supports. To create these parts, we used advanced and basic aspects of maya such as shapes, translate, rotate, scale, channel/input changes, extrude, bevel, multicut, connect, curve/sweep mesh, booleans, etc. I customized the materials and its colors and also added lights to my scene. We did this to make the rendering experience more realistic and changed colors to not make it basic. Once every part was completed , I moved onto animating. The rigging required me to make the arm contain the following: arm, wrist, palm, middle1, middle2, middle3, and middle4. I also had to animate the arm to have a waving animation(left and right). Once I completed the parts, It was time to animate. The animation required me to have at least 3 shots and 2 cuts. I had to keep this in mind while animating. I animated 3 shots from the front, side, and directly from the top. The cuts were from in between the front shot and side shot, and between the side shot and top shot. I started at frame one and put every key frame around 30-40 frames apart. This allowed my animation to flow pretty smoothly and not so quickly. My entire animation consisted of 200 frames.
